How Often Should You Visit a Dentist?

Purple Rain Dental Clinic · February 20, 2025

Regular dental visits are key to maintaining good oral health and catching problems early. At Purple Rain Dental Clinic, we often get asked how often patients should come in. Here are general guidelines.

For Most Adults

We recommend visiting a dentist at least twice a year (every six months). These visits typically include a check-up and professional cleaning. They help prevent cavities, gum disease, and other issues, and allow your dentist to spot any concerns before they become serious.

When You Might Need More Frequent Visits

Some adults may need to come more often, for example:

  • If you have a history of gum disease or many fillings
  • If you are diabetic or have other conditions that affect oral health
  • If you smoke or use other tobacco products
  • If you are pregnant
  • If you have a weakened immune system

Your dentist can give you a personalised schedule based on your oral and general health.

Children

Children should start seeing a dentist when their first tooth appears or by their first birthday. After that, a check-up every six months is usually recommended. Regular visits help children get used to the dental clinic and allow early detection of decay or alignment issues.

Don’t Skip Visits

Even if your teeth feel fine, plaque and tartar can build up, and small problems may not cause symptoms until they are more advanced. Scheduling routine visits at Purple Rain Dental Clinic helps keep your smile healthy for the long term.
